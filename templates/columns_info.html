Heart Disease Prediction - Input Parameters

Summary of Tests Needed

  • No Tests Required: Age, Sex
  • Basic Tests: Blood Pressure, Fasting Blood Sugar
  • Specialized Tests: ECG (Resting and Stress), Cholesterol Test, Exercise Stress Test, Angiography or Fluoroscopy, Thalassemia Screening

1. Age

  • What it Means: The age of the patient in years.
  • How to Determine: This is the patient’s current age. No special test is needed.

2. Sex

  • What it Means: Gender of the patient.
  • Categories: 0 = Female, 1 = Male
  • How to Determine: Based on the patient’s biological sex as per medical records.

3. Chest Pain Type (cp)

  • What it Means: Describes the type of chest pain experienced.
  • Categories:
    • 0: Typical angina (chest pain related to exertion, relieved by rest or medication)
    • 1: Atypical angina (chest discomfort not typical for angina)
    • 2: Non-anginal pain (pain not related to heart conditions)
    • 3: Asymptomatic (no chest pain)
  • How to Determine: A doctor determines this based on symptoms and patient history.

4. Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps)

  • What it Means: Blood pressure measured while at rest, expressed in mmHg.
  • How to Determine: Measured using a blood pressure cuff and monitor in a clinic.
  • Examples:
    • Normal: 120/80 mmHg
    • Hypertension: 140/90 mmHg or higher
    • Hypotension: Less than 90/60 mmHg

5. Serum Cholesterol (chol)

  • What it Means: The total cholesterol level in the blood, measured in mg/dl.
  • How to Determine: A blood test is required, typically after fasting for 8-12 hours.
  • Examples:
    • Desirable: Less than 200 mg/dl
    • High: Greater than 240 mg/dl

6. Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs)

  • What it Means: Indicates if fasting blood sugar is normal or elevated.
  • Categories:
    • 0: Below 120 mg/dl (Normal)
    • 1: Above 120 mg/dl (High)
  • How to Determine: Blood test after fasting for 8-12 hours.

7. Resting Electrocardiographic Results (restecg)

  • What it Means: Results of an ECG performed at rest.
  • Categories:
    • 0: Normal
    • 1: ST-T wave abnormality
    • 2: Probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy
  • How to Determine: An ECG test performed by a doctor.

8. Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach)

  • What it Means: The highest heart rate achieved during exercise or stress testing.
  • How to Determine: Measured during a stress test.
  • Example:
    • For a 50-year-old, the maximum heart rate is approximately 170 bpm.

9. Exercise Induced Angina (exang)

  • What it Means: Indicates whether the patient experiences chest pain during physical activity:
  • Categories:
    • 0: No angina
    • 1: Angina (chest pain)
  • How to Determine: Assessed during an exercise stress test by a doctor.

10. ST Depression Induced by Exercise Relative to Rest (oldpeak)

  • What it Means: Measures the difference in the ST segment of an ECG between rest and exercise, indicating heart stress.
  • How to Determine: Measured during an exercise ECG test.
  • Examples:
    • Normal Value: No significant depression.
    • Abnormal Value: ST depression of 1.5 mm or greater may indicate coronary artery disease or other heart problems.

11. Slope of the Peak Exercise ST Segment (slope)

  • What it Means: Indicates the slope of the ST segment during peak exercise.
  • Categories:
    • 0: Upsloping (less concerning)
    • 1: Flat (moderate risk)
    • 2: Downsloping (higher risk)
  • How to Determine: Determined during an exercise ECG test by a cardiologist.

12. Number of Major Vessels Colored by Fluoroscopy (ca)

  • What it Means: Indicates the number of major blood vessels (0–3) visible during fluoroscopy (an imaging technique).
  • How to Determine: Performed during an angiography or fluoroscopy procedure where contrast dye is injected, and images are taken.
  • Examples:
    • 0: No major vessels blocked.
    • 1–3: Indicates the number of major vessels with reduced blood flow.

13. Thalassemia (thal)

  • What it Means: A genetic blood disorder detected via tests.
  • Categories:
    • 0: Normal
    • 1: Fixed defect (permanent heart damage)
    • 2: Reversible defect (temporary heart issues)
  • How to Determine: Diagnosed using specialized blood tests, such as a hemoglobin electrophoresis test.
